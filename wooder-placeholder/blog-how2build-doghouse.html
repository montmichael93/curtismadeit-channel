How to Build the Big Dog House

  • Home
  • All Articles
  • Admin
  • Comment 03
  • January 17, 2019

1
Once you’ve determined the size of the structure you are looking to build for your dog, it’s time to start your material selection. I’m going to recommend cedar fence pickets and 2 x 4’s from your local home improvement store. These materials are likely the cheapest and most readily available construction materials in your area. Start by creating yourself a base using 2x4’s.

2
I’m going to recommend outdoor rated screws or a brad nailer(with glue maybe) for this project. This will be the support for the floor of your structure and will help keep our animal’s feet dry due to being raised off of the ground. Cover the base/frame with cedar fence pickets that are cut to the length of your base. I would recommend cutting the ‘dog eared’ part of the picket off so all your corners are square.

3
Take the time to use an orbital sander on the deck of the dog house. We want the floor as smooth as it can be for our doggie friends. Walls are then constructed. For this build, I cut the front and back wall pieces slightly longer than the length of the base. These pieces were secured together on a 2x4 wall support, and the wall support was secured to the base using a method known as ‘toe-nailing’.

4
The longer front and back wall pieces provided an overlap in the corners so as to hopefully keep the cold winter wind out. The side pieces were attached to the front and back wall supports from the sides of the dog house. The front wall pieces were cut to allow for an opening for entry and exit to the dog house and an extra wall support was placed inside of the opening to help maintain structural integrity. Cedar fence pickets aren’t thick enough to be able to span 48” without bowing, so roof supports were added.

5
These were 2x4’s as well and we added cut out blocks under the supports to further reenforce the roof. No stain or finish was applied to this one as it lives under a patio, out of direct sunlight and the elements, but with a properly prepared surface, you can take your creativity to an entirely different stratosphere by customizing the look and feel of your friends new hangout with any number of different stains and paints available.

In conclusion

Check your local home improvement store for available options. Looking back at this build now, this is a pretty primitive and beginner level build, but this should serve as some kind of foundation to help spawn new ideas for your own creation. Let me know how I can help you with yours!
